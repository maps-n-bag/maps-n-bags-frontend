Whispers of the
Zermatt Mist

Capturing the early morning ascent through the larch forests and the first glimpse of the Matterhorn's jagged silhouette.

“

The air at five thousand feet is different. It tastes of crisp pine and the ancient silence of stone. I started the trek long before the first café opened in the village, following the scent of the damp earth as the stars began to pale.

notes

Notes from the Road

Don't forget to pack the extra layer for the descent. The temperature drops sharply once the sun dips behind the North face. Met an old mountaineer named Klaus at the mid-station. He told me about the 1974 blizzard.

By mid-morning, the valley was a sea of clouds. I sat on a limestone outcrop, the world below hidden under a wooly blanket, feeling like an inhabitant of some floating island. This is why we climb—not for the summit, but for the clarity of the vertical perspective.
